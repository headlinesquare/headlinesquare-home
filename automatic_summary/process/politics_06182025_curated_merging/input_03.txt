## 1. Foreign Policy and National Security

### 1.1 Iran Crisis: Escalation and Diplomacy
The executive branch is actively managing a military and diplomatic standoff with Iran. President Trump has reportedly approved military attack plans but is withholding the final order, leveraging the threat of force to pressure Tehran on its nuclear program [1]. The President has publicly stated that Iran "missed a chance for a deal" and made a "very late" request to the White House, the details of which remain unspecified [2]. The crisis is also creating complex domestic political cross-currents. Polling indicates that a majority of the President's supporters oppose joining Israel in a potential conflict with Iran, even as some media outlets reportedly advocate for war [5]. Strategically, the vulnerability of U.S. troops in the Middle East to potential Iranian retaliation remains a primary concern for defense planners [6].
