## 2. Economic Policy & Impacts

### 2.1 GDP Contraction & Tariff Effects
The U.S. economy contracted 0.3% in the first quarter of 2025, marking the first economic decline since 2022 [1][2]. Multiple sources attribute this contraction to disruptions caused by the administration's tariff policies [3][4]. President Trump has blamed the previous administration's "overhang" and stated economic growth will "take a while" [5]. UPS announced 20,000 job cuts and closure of 164 buildings, explicitly citing reduced Amazon deliveries and the impact of "new or increased tariffs" [6]. Consumer outlook has reached its lowest point since 2011 amid mounting tariff fears [7].

### 2.2 Financial Markets & Trade Strategy
Financial markets are experiencing significant volatility, with turmoil spreading to European markets as investors seek "tariff-proof" investments [1]. A senior Trump advisor has met with hedge funds and asset managers attempting to calm investors following market turbulence [2]. Goldman Sachs warns foreign demand for U.S. assets will likely decline without significant dollar depreciation [3], despite Treasury officials claiming "Main Street investors hold on tight out of trust in President Trump" [4]. Trump has expressed confidence that China would bear the economic burden of tariffs, claiming China "probably will eat those tariffs" [5].

## 3. Immigration & Border Enforcement

### 3.1 Enforcement Operations & Legal Challenges
The administration's immigration enforcement faces judicial intervention, with a judge ordering the White House to restore legal aid to unaccompanied migrant children [1] and expressing skepticism toward arguments that federal courts lack authority to review the President's border declaration [2]. ICE operations drew criticism after agents mistakenly raided the home of U.S. citizens in Oklahoma, with family members reportedly stating "We're citizens!" and claiming they were "traumatized for life" [3]. Federal authorities have revealed details of how an immigration squad specifically "targeted thousands of foreign students" [4], while a judge criticized the administration's approach to student visa terminations as "arbitrary and capricious" [5].

### 3.2 High-Profile Immigration Cases
The case of Kilmar Abrego Garcia, deported to El Salvador, has become particularly contentious. President Trump has stated he "could" bring Abrego Garcia back to the United States but chooses not to [1], while Governor Kristi Noem declared if Abrego Garcia were returned, "we would immediately deport him again" [2]. Trump defended what critics claim is an edited photo related to Abrego Garcia [3]. Separately, Trump's border pick faces accusations of participating in a "cover-up" regarding the death of a man beaten by U.S. agents [4].
