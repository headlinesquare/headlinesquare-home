## 1. Economic Policy and Performance

### 1.1 Tariffs and Trade War Impacts
The US economy has contracted in the first quarter of 2025, with multiple sources attributing this downturn to President Trump's tariff policies. Reuters reports that tariffs have "unleashed a flood of imports" [1], while economic reversal began in the run-up to tariff implementation [2]. Recession fears are mounting [3] and major employers are announcing layoffs, with UPS citing Trump's tariffs as it plans to cut 20,000 jobs [4]. On the international front, China has created a list of US-made goods exempt from its retaliatory 125% tariffs [5].

### 1.2 Economic Indicators and Administration Response
Economic indicators show private payroll growth slowed to just 62,000 in April, well below expectations [1]. The administration has attributed these declines to the previous administration, with President Trump blaming a Biden "overhang" for GDP contraction [2]. This represents a messaging shift, as Trump is now pinning stock market struggles on Biden just weeks after taking credit for market performance [3]. Trade adviser Peter Navarro has dismissed concerns about the GDP drop, stating "We really like where we're at now" [4].
