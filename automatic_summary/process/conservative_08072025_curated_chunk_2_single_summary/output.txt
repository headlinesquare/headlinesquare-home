## 1. The Trump Administration: Policy & Personnel

### 1.1 Presidential Orders & Regulatory Actions
The Trump administration is actively using executive authority to implement core policy objectives across education, fiscal management, and financial regulation. A forthcoming executive order is set to directly challenge affirmative action policies in higher education by compelling colleges and universities to formally prove they do not consider race as a factor in student admissions, presumably as a condition for receiving federal funding [1]. This represents a significant federal intervention into university administration. In parallel, the White House has released a fact sheet detailing a new initiative to "Stop Wasteful Grantmaking," signaling a broad effort to scrutinize and curtail specific areas of federal spending [2]. Expanding the administration's focus to the private sector, President Trump has also issued a directive ordering federal regulators to launch a formal investigation into the financial industry. This probe will specifically examine widespread allegations from conservatives that banks and other financial institutions have engaged in politically motivated discrimination against them [3]. Together, these actions illustrate a multi-front strategy to enact the administration's agenda through direct regulatory and executive power.
