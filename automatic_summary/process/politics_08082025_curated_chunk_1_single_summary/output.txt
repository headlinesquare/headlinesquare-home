## 3. Economic Policy & Trade

### 3.1. Tariffs & International Trade Disputes
The administration is actively expanding its tariff-based trade strategy, impacting key allies and global alignments. President Trump has signed an order imposing a 15 percent tariff on Israeli imports [1] and is threatening similar actions against India, a move that Bloomberg describes as upending decades of U.S. policy [2]. The economic consequences for U.S. companies are significant; Ford Motor Company reportedly paid $800 million in tariff-related costs in just three months, despite building most of its cars in America [3]. This broad policy is creating new geopolitical dynamics, with some analysis suggesting the tariffs may be pushing rivals India and China closer together [4].
