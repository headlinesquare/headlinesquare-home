## 1. Executive Branch: Domestic Policy and Power

### 1.1 Federal Law Enforcement and National Guard Mobilization
The Trump administration has launched a significant expansion of federal power into U.S. cities through a pair of executive actions. One order establishes "specialized" National Guard units to directly address crime in urban areas [1], while a second order takes aim at eliminating cashless bail policies nationwide [2, 3]. These unilateral moves have prompted immediate and forceful opposition from state leaders. In a clear challenge to the administration, Illinois Governor J.B. Pritzker has publicly declared any deployment of military troops to Chicago "illegal" and vowed to prevent it, issuing a direct warning to the President: "Do not come to Chicago" with such forces [4, 5]. The implementation of these policies is already creating public anxiety, with one report noting that parents in Washington are "on edge" as schools reopen amidst the new presence of armed patrols [6]. To advance this agenda, Attorney General Pam Bondi is scheduled to meet with the NYPD in a meeting explicitly intended to implement the President's anti-cashless bail agenda [7].

### 1.2 Executive Actions on First Amendment Issues
The administration is taking direct aim at First Amendment protections concerning both symbolic speech and the press. President Trump has initiated executive action to ban the burning of the U.S. flag [1], ordering a federal crackdown on the act [2]. This move directly challenges established legal precedent, as it defies a standing Supreme Court ruling that protects flag burning as a form of constitutional speech [3]. Concurrently, the President has explicitly threatened the broadcast licenses of major news networks, specifically NBC and ABC, in response to their news coverage [4]. While the threats themselves have raised alarms, some media experts have expressed an even deeper concern about the potential for "capitulation of corporate owners" of news outlets, suggesting that corporate self-censorship in the face of presidential pressure could pose a greater threat to press freedom [5].

At the same time, internal staffers at the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) are raising alarms, alleging that recent budget cuts have significantly increased public risk ahead of potential disasters [4].
