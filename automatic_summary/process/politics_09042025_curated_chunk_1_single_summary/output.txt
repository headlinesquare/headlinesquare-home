On the Senate Finance Committee, Senator Ron Wyden is formally demanding Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent release Epstein-related financial records that are allegedly being withheld [1]. In the House, Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene reported facing "a lot" of pushback from the White House over her discharge petition to force a vote on the matter [2]. This issue is fueling significant dissension against GOP leadership. Reports indicate a group of Republicans are publicly challenging their party's leaders on the matter, with Republican women reportedly at the forefront of this resistance to President Trump's position [3]. While some members like Rep. Nancy Mace are said to be defending the President, a House Democrat has labeled the administration's handling of the situation as "pathetic."

## 2. Executive Branch and Presidential Actions

### 2.1 Immigration Policy and Enforcement

The administration is executing a significant escalation of its immigration crackdown. President Trump has officially ended Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for hundreds of thousands of Venezuelans, stripping them of deportation protections [1]. This policy is supported by intensified and controversial field operations. Reports indicate ICE agents are now interviewing parents of unaccompanied minors who are seeking reunification [2]. Simultaneously, "harrowing" court filings have revealed details of the administration's child deportation flights, describing fear and stress among the children. The administration is also opening a new ICE facility at a "notorious Louisiana prison." These enforcement actions continue despite a court order, which is reportedly not being enforced, that was intended to bar ICE from targeting individuals based on race [3]. One analysis suggests the crackdown is putting the U.S. on track for its first-ever population decline.
