## 1. Trump Administration Policies and Actions

### 1.1 Immigration and Travel Restrictions

The Trump administration has intensified immigration enforcement activities, with The Guardian characterizing it as "an unprecedented crusade against legal immigrants" [1]. This approach has encountered judicial resistance, evidenced by a court order mandating the administration to permit a previously deported Guatemalan man to return to the United States [2].

The State Department has implemented enhanced vetting requirements specifically for foreign visitors to Harvard University, who must now be screened for "hostile attitudes towards US culture" [3]. Enforcement priorities appear to include long-term residents with pending legal status applications, as demonstrated by ICE's detention of a mother who has lived in the US for 22 years while her visa application remains in process [4]. Senior advisor Stephen Miller has reportedly expressed frustration over "insufficient arrests in ICE operations" [5]. In a related development, another report highlights that Trump is cracking down further on foreign nationals—underscoring his administration's aggressive stance [6].
