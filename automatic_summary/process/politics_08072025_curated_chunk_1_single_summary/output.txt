## 1. International Relations and Global Trade

### 1.1 Sweeping Tariffs Implemented, Escalating Trade War
The administration has enacted a significant escalation of its global trade policy, with multiple reports confirming new, "staggering" tariffs have officially taken effect [1]. This move reportedly raises U.S. import taxes to their highest levels since the Great Depression [2]. The policy is broad, applying to dozens of countries and representing a widening of the ongoing trade war. Specific actions include levying new tariffs on India for its continued purchase of Russian oil [3], accompanied by public vows to impose secondary sanctions on other nations, including potentially China, for similar energy deals. In a targeted measure aimed at reshoring manufacturing, President Trump has also threatened a tariff of "roughly 100%" on foreign-made semiconductors unless their parent companies commit to building production facilities within the United States [4]. Initial economic indicators suggest negative consequences, with one Republican official claiming their state's GDP has plunged by 6% during a "'Troubled time'", and other reports pointing to emerging "cracks in the U.S. economy" as the tariffs go into effect [5].

A notable shift in U.S. foreign policy is underway, with plans to ease human rights criticisms of El Salvador, Israel, and Russia [1]. In a concrete example of this policy, the U.S. has announced its backing for the "indefinite reelection" of El Salvador's Bukele, explicitly rejecting comparisons to "dictatorial regimes" [3]. On the geopolitical stage, high-level talks are imminent, with the Kremlin announcing that President Trump is scheduled to meet with President Putin in the coming days [2]. Concurrently, Secretary of State Rubio has stated that an individual named Witkoff is returning with a "Russia-Ukraine ceasefire proposal," signaling potential new diplomatic overtures [4]. In Europe, a Trump-backed candidate, Karol Nawrocki, has reportedly secured the presidency in Poland, a development that is expected to steer the country toward a more nationalist political course [5].

## 3. Domestic Policy and National Affairs

### 3.1 New Census Ordered to Exclude Non-Citizens Amid Redistricting Battles
President Trump has issued a directive to the Commerce Department to begin preparations for a new U.S. census, a move with profound implications for congressional apportionment [1]. A key and unprecedented component of this order is the demand that the census must exclude non-citizens who are either "unauthorized residents" or have "no legal status" from the official population count [2]. This federal action, which would fundamentally alter how political power is apportioned, is unfolding as states engage in their own contentious electoral map-drawing processes. Notably, Texas is advancing a redistricting plan designed to create five new Republican-held House seats before the 2026 midterm elections, a strategy that reports suggest other states may emulate [3].
