## 1. Foreign Policy & National Security

### 1.1 The U.S.-Russia Summit in Alaska

The impending summit between President Trump and Russian President Putin is fraught with high stakes and conflicting signals from the administration. The meeting is set for a U.S. military base in Alaska, a location chosen for its symbolic value as a historic outpost used to counter Russian military activity [1]. In a deliberate move to manage public and international expectations, the White House has set a low bar for the summit's potential outcomes [2].

A complex and contradictory diplomatic picture is emerging. President Trump has reportedly given private assurances to Ukrainian President Zelenskyy and other allies that he will not negotiate away any territory, a core concern for Kyiv [3]. This is paired with a stern public warning of "severe consequences" if President Putin does not consent to a ceasefire [4]. However, these strong stances are significantly undermined by reports that the administration's top Russia specialists will be absent from the talks. The assertion that there will be "no experts in the room" has amplified concerns among observers that the President could be persuaded into unvetted agreements without the necessary counsel [5]. Compounding these anxieties are reports that President Trump is considering an offer to President Putin involving natural resources off the coast of Alaska, a potential concession that has already provoked significant political fury [6]. A joint press conference is scheduled to follow the conclusion of the talks [7].
