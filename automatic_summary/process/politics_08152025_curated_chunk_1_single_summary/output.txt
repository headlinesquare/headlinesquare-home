## 1. Governance and Executive Action

### 1.1. Federal Takeover of Washington, D.C. Police

The executive branch has asserted direct federal control over the Washington, D.C. Metropolitan Police Department, marking an unprecedented expansion of federal power over a local jurisdiction. The action was formalized when Attorney General Pam Bondi ordered the D.C. mayor to recognize the head of the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) as an “emergency police commissioner” [1], with the administration subsequently claiming the full policing powers of the D.C. police chief [2]. One direct operational consequence has been the administration overruling D.C. police on local immigration enforcement policies [3]. This federal move has been met with immediate and forceful resistance. The D.C. Attorney General has filed a lawsuit to block what is described in multiple reports as a "hostile takeover" of the city’s police force [4, 5]. Concurrently, the federal presence is tangible on the streets, with D.C. residents protesting the deployment of the National Guard and federal agents who are now operating checkpoints [6]. However, despite the administration's new authority, reports from the ground indicate the city's highest-crime neighborhoods have "yet to see Trump crackdown" [7].

Separately, a headline concerning federal prosecution priorities states that the office of U.S. Attorney Pirro has admitted that a grand jury twice refused to bring charges related to ICE interference [5].
