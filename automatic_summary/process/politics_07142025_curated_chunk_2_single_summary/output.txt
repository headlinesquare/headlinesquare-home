## 1. Foreign Policy and National Security

### 1.1. Ukraine Conflict and Russia Ultimatum

In a significant and abrupt reversal of its previous stance, the Trump administration has committed to providing substantial military support for Ukraine and has issued a stark ultimatum to the Russian Federation. This major policy shift occurred following a direct meeting between President Trump and the NATO chief to discuss weaponry for Kyiv [1]. The administration has now announced it is sending Patriot missile systems to Ukraine and has authorized "billions of dollars" in further armaments through a new, parallel deal with the NATO alliance [2, 3]. This development represents a notable change in presidential posture, with one report highlighting that the President's "rhetoric about Zelenskyy and Putin has evolved" [4].

This military support is strategically coupled with a severe economic threat designed to force a diplomatic resolution. The administration has given the Russian Federation a 50-day deadline to reach a peace agreement with Ukraine [5]. If this deadline passes without a settlement, the White House has announced it will impose crippling 100% tariffs on all Russian goods. Furthermore, the U.S. will levy secondary sanctions against any country that continues to purchase Russian oil, a move intended to cut off a primary source of Moscow's revenue [6]. This dual-pronged strategy of arming Ukraine while threatening Russia's economic lifelines marks a critical turning point in the administration's foreign policy.
