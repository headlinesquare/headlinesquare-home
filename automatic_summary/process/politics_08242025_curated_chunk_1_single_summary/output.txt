This convergence of federal planning and high-level state resistance signals an imminent and potentially severe jurisdictional conflict.

### 4.1. National Redistricting Battle Intensifies

A centrally coordinated national redistricting effort is escalating into a primary political battle over the future composition of Congress. The Trump administration is spearheading a push that, according to a detailed Reuters analysis, could potentially secure "decades of Republican rule" over the U.S. House of Representatives [1]. Texas is a key battleground, where state lawmakers have already approved a new congressional map explicitly designed to "secure Trump's hold on Congress" [2]. Vice President JD Vance has publicly defended this nationwide strategy, framing it as an effort to make the electoral maps "'more fair'" [3]. These moves are being met with significant state-level resistance. In a major counter-effort, a redistricting plan in California backed by Gavin Newsom is now set to be decided directly by voters, posing a potential policy divergence [4]. This intensifying "redistricting war" is reportedly fueling public discontent, with one account capturing voter frustration with the sentiment that "this isn’t how a well-run republic runs" [5].
