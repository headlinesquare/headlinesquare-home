## 4. Electoral Process & Governance

### 4.1 Redistricting and Electoral Maps

Redistricting has escalated into a primary national political battleground, with actions being taken that could have profound, long-term consequences for the balance of power in Washington. A detailed Reuters analysis concludes that the current redistricting push, spearheaded by the Trump administration, holds the potential to entrench Republican control of the U.S. House of Representatives for decades to come [1]. This strategic effort has moved from theory to practice in Texas, where state lawmakers have officially approved a new congressional map that news reports explicitly state was "designed to secure Trump's hold on Congress" [2]. Vice President JD Vance has publicly defended these Republican-led efforts nationwide, framing them not as a power grab but as a move to make electoral districts "more fair" [3]. In sharp contrast, a different approach is being pursued in California, where a redistricting plan championed by Gavin Newsom is now set to be decided directly by voters [4]. The intensity of these conflicts is creating a volatile political environment, with some reports quoting "frustrated voters" who feel their democratic process is being undermined by partisan warfare [5].
