## 1. Executive Branch: Power, Personnel, and Conduct

### 1.1 Federal Intervention in D.C. and Other Cities

The Trump administration has directed the deployment of the National Guard onto the streets of Washington, D.C., following what reports describe as a "police takeover" of the city [1]. The stated mission of the arriving troops is to combat crime and reduce homelessness [2]. However, the factual basis for this significant intervention is sharply contested, with reports indicating President Trump publicly misstated crime statistics for the city to justify the move [3]. This action is perceived by some Black leaders as a politically calculated maneuver targeting "blue cities," with an explicit concern that New York and Chicago may be subsequent targets for similar federal takeovers [4, 5]. The deployment is provoking widespread alarm among civil rights and housing advocates, who fear a policy of mass incarceration for homeless individuals [6]. In the face of this escalating federal pressure, D.C. Mayor Bowser has reportedly adopted a new, more conciliatory tone in her interactions with President Trump [7], while also renewing calls for the U.S. capital to become the 51st state [8].
