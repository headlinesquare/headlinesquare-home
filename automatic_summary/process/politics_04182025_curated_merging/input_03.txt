## 1. Immigration and Deportation

### 1.1 Deportation Policies & Legal Challenges
The Trump administration's deportation initiatives face significant legal opposition. A federal appeals court has denied the administration's attempt to end deportation protections for Venezuelans [1], while a U.S. judge halted the plan for rapid deportations to third countries [2]. Cases of American citizens being wrongfully detained by ICE have emerged, including one individual held despite proving U.S. birth [3]. Internationally, a legal group has sued Costa Rica alleging violations of 81 children's rights after their deportation by the Trump administration [4]. The administration has taken a hardline stance, with one Trump adviser suggesting critics of deportations may be committing a crime [5].

### 1.2 The Abrego Garcia Case
The deportation of Kilmar Abrego Garcia to El Salvador has become a diplomatic focal point following Senator Chris Van Hollen's visit. Van Hollen reported that Abrego Garcia described being "traumatized" at the CECOT detention facility before being relocated [1]. The Senator revealed El Salvador's government staged elements of their meeting, including arranged "margaritas" for a photo opportunity that nobody drank [2]. The White House has dismissed concerns, boasting that Abrego Garcia is "never coming back" to the US [3], while President Trump attacked Van Hollen as a "grandstander" who "looked like a fool" [4].

## 2. Executive Power and Restructuring

### 2.1 Presidential Authority Expansion
President Trump is significantly expanding executive authority through multiple mechanisms. The administration has placed more federal civil servants directly under presidential control [1], and is implementing Schedule F to make it easier to fire certain federal workers by removing civil service protections [2]. Within the administration, deliberations about potentially invoking the Insurrection Act are ongoing, though neither the Pentagon nor Department of Homeland Security currently recommend this action [3].

### 2.2 Federal Workforce & Agency Changes
Significant disruption is occurring within federal agencies. Trump's pick for acting commissioner is out at the IRS just days after appointment [1], reportedly due to a power struggle between Treasury Secretary and Elon Musk [2]. Defense Department leadership is in flux, with the Secretary's chief of staff departing amid Pentagon turmoil [3]. Federal agencies face operational disruptions due to funding cuts, with the Consumer Product Safety Commission stopping collection of "swaths of data" [5] and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau reportedly "slashed to the bone" [6].
