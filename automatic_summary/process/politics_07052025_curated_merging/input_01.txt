Opposition is forming rapidly and is notably bipartisan. Democrats have begun to mobilize against the "massively unpopular" bill, with a strategy that includes highlighting personal stories of Americans who rely on aid programs like Medicaid [3]. Reflecting this, the New Democrat Coalition condemned the package as the "most damaging legislation for working families in modern American history" [4]. Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez additionally warned of an impending "ICE ‘Explosion’" as a direct consequence of the bill's funding measures [5]. Criticism is also emerging from the right; Senator Rand Paul published an opinion piece explaining his vote against what he termed the "'Big, Not-So-Beautiful Bill'" [6], and a New York Times headline suggests some conservatives feel they have become "prisoners of their own tax cuts" [7].
