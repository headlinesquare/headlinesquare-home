## 1. Executive Branch and Federal Governance

### 1.1 Supreme Court Ruling Enables Mass Dismissal of Federal Workforce; Key Appointments Made

The Supreme Court has issued a landmark ruling backing President Trump's authority to conduct mass firings of federal workers, a decision that has reportedly instilled widespread fear across the civil service [1, 2]. Headlines indicate the Court has "disabused federal workers who thought their jobs were safe" [2]. This new authority is being enacted swiftly. The administration is reportedly planning the dismissal of 2,145 employees at the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) [3].

This move has prompted analysis focused on the reasoning behind assigning a Transportation Secretary to lead the nation's space agency [5]. In a separate but related development, the President's nominee to lead the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) has pledged to use new executive powers to restaff the weather service.

## 3. Domestic Policy and Legislation

### 3.1 "Big Beautiful Bill" Contains Major Cuts to Social Programs

President Trump's signature legislative package, referred to as the "big beautiful bill," contains significant reforms to federal social programs. The bill mandates cuts to food stamps that will affect millions of recipients, with one analysis finding that an average family could see its benefits reduced by $146 per month [1]. The impact of these cuts is projected to be particularly severe in Republican-leaning states that have a high dependency on the program [2]. While implementing cuts, the bill also reportedly establishes a rural health fund, although headlines only question its function rather than providing detail [3]. In an unusual development, Senator Josh Hawley, who voted for the bill, is now reportedly attempting to block a key Medicaid cut contained within the legislation from taking effect [4].
