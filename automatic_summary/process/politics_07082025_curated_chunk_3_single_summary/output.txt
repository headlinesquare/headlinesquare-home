## 1. Executive Branch: Powers, Governance & Personnel

### 1.1 Federal Workforce Reorganization & SCOTUS Ruling
The Supreme Court has issued a landmark ruling that permits the Trump administration to proceed with its plans for mass firings and "sweeping workforce cuts" [1, 2]. Multiple major news outlets report this decision clears a path for the administration to resume its efforts to "dramatically reshape the federal government" and implement "large-scale government agency staff cuts" [3, 4]. While this provides a significant green light for the executive's restructuring agenda, the authority is not absolute. A critical check has emerged from the lower courts, where a U.S. judge ruled that recent layoffs at the Department of Health and Human Services were “likely unlawful and must be halted” [5]. This creates a direct constitutional conflict, pitting the President’s reorganization plans against judicial oversight, with the final scope of the administration’s power still subject to ongoing legal challenges.

## 2. Economic & Fiscal Policy

### 2.1 Tariffs & International Trade Policy
The administration is pursuing a highly protectionist trade policy marked by significant new tariff threats. President Trump has personally announced plans for a 50% tariff on all copper imports [1] and has suggested that tariffs on pharmaceuticals could be raised to as high as 200% [2]. The scope of these actions is broad, with the President threatening over a dozen countries with new tariffs that would be implemented by August 1 [3]. This aggressive posture is creating significant uncertainty for existing trade relationships, with one report questioning whether the planned “big, beautiful” India-US trade deal is now in jeopardy [4]. An opinion piece suggests that while these policies may affect Wall Street, "everyday citizens will be hit hardest" [5].
