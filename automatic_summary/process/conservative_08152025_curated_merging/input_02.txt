## 2. Federal Governance & Executive Actions

### 2.1 D.C. Crime Crackdown and Federal Police Takeover

The administration has initiated an unprecedented federal takeover of law enforcement in Washington, D.C., citing a public safety crisis. The action began with an order from Attorney General Pam Bondi to replace the city's Police Chief with a federal official [1]. This has created a critical jurisdictional clash, with the sitting D.C. Police Chief Smith decrying the move in the strongest terms, stating, "I have never seen a single government action that would cause a greater threat to law" [2]. When asked if Democrats would support the president's action, Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer responded with a profanity-laced refusal [3].

Operationally, federal agents are now in command of the D.C. police force and have already made nearly 200 arrests [4]. As part of the new federal oversight, the task force has also reportedly streamlined the city's gun permit process down to a matter of days [5]. Proponents frame the intervention as a necessary measure, circulating supportive op-eds [6] and historical footage of Joe Biden warning about D.C.'s crime problems in 1992 [7] to counter criticism.
