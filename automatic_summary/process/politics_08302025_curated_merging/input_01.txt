**Executive Summary**

The Trump administration faced major setbacks in federal courts today, with judges striking down key tariff policies as an overstep of authority and halting the expansion of fast-track deportations over due process concerns. Personnel turmoil continues, as the SSA's chief data officer resigned after filing a whistleblower complaint against DOGE, and the appointment of a new, non-medical acting director at the CDC sparked widespread criticism. Tensions between federal and state governments are escalating, with Illinois's governor warning against military deployment to Chicago. Abroad, the administration's hardline diplomacy is causing friction with allies and international bodies.

## 2. Judicial and Legal Affairs

First, a U.S. appeals court broadly invalidated many of the administration's tariffs [1]. In rulings carried by multiple major outlets, the court found the tariffs illegal because the President had "overstepped his authority" in implementing them [2, 3]. In a separate and equally significant case, a federal judge blocked the administration from expanding fast-track deportations across the nation, citing "due process concerns" [4]. One report characterized the deportation ruling as a judicial block on a "pillar of Trump's mass deportation campaign" [5].
