## 1. The Judiciary, Executive Power, and Federal Law
### 1.1. Supreme Court Restricts Nationwide Injunctions
In a landmark decision with profound implications for executive power, the Supreme Court has significantly curtailed the ability of lower federal courts to issue nationwide injunctions that block administration policies. In a widely reported ruling, the court’s conservative majority ended the power of federal judges to impose such broad orders, representing a major procedural victory for the executive branch [1, 2]. This move fundamentally alters a key tool used by opponents to halt federal policies across the entire country, limiting lower court orders to their specific jurisdictions instead [3]. The decision directly constrains the power of any single district court to block President Trump’s agenda on a national scale [4].

### 1.2. Ruling on Birthright Citizenship Policy
The Supreme Court’s decision on nationwide injunctions was delivered in the context of, and applied directly to, legal challenges against President Trump’s order on birthright citizenship [1]. The Court’s action allows the administration to partially enforce its order by lifting the broad, nationwide injunctions that had previously blocked it [2]. While the justices did not rule on the ultimate legality of the birthright citizenship policy itself, the case, cited as *TRUMP, PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, ET AL. v. CASA, INC., ET AL.*, has been sent back to lower courts with new instructions [3]. This ruling is widely interpreted as clearing a significant procedural hurdle for the administration's plans to limit birthright citizenship, with one MSNBC analysis describing it as a "permission slip for mass deportation" [4].

### 1.3. Rulings on Healthcare and Social Programs

Conversely, in a separate 6-3 decision involving a case from South Carolina, the Court ruled that states have a path to defund Planned Parenthood by barring access for Medicaid patients [2].
