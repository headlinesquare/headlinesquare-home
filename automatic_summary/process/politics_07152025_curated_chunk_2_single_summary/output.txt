## 1. Economic Policy and Performance

### 1.1. Inflation, Tariffs, and Consumer Prices
Economic reports for June 2025 indicate an acceleration in U.S. inflation. Official data shows a 2.7% annual rate [1], marking the highest level in four months [2]. A strong consensus is emerging across multiple analyses that directly links this inflationary pressure to the administration's tariff policies. Reports state that the effects of the tariffs are now beginning to "show in prices" [3] and are starting to "bite" American consumers [4]. The connection is so clear that some analyses explicitly declare, "It’s Official: Trump’s Tariffs Are Driving Up Inflation" [5]. This body of reporting presents a unified conclusion: the administration's trade strategy is a primary and observable driver of rising costs for American households.

The public debate highlights a fundamental conflict over the independence of the central bank and the potential for political pressure to influence monetary policy.

This signals a strategic realignment from a key trading partner. The President's "tariff letters" are reportedly causing "a stir among world leaders," indicating widespread international concern [3]. The impact is also being felt directly in supply chains. An industry lobby reports that American meatpackers are rethinking beef export strategies to the United States as a direct result of the new duties, a tangible consequence for the agricultural sector [4].
