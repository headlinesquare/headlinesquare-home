## 1. The Administration's "Megabill"

### 1.1. Impacts on healthcare, social programs, and local services
The Administration's primary legislative focus, a package referred to as the "megabill" or "Big Beautiful Bill," is nearing final passage. Its central provision is reported to be the "largest Medicaid cuts in US History," a move that has prompted dire warnings that "People Will Suffer, People Will Die" [1]. Analysis suggests these cuts could strip millions of their health insurance, creating a significant public health crisis, with Washington state reportedly being the "No. 1 target" of these reductions [2, 3, 4]. The consequences are already being felt on the ground, as a rural clinic in southwest Nebraska has announced its closure, explicitly citing the expected Medicaid cuts as the reason [5]. The bill's impact extends to other social programs; Pennsylvania's Governor, Josh Shapiro, has publicly stated he is unsure if the SNAP program can continue to exist in his state under the new framework [6]. The legislation is a complex package that reportedly combines these social spending cuts with tax cuts for the wealthy and increased funding for Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) [7].
