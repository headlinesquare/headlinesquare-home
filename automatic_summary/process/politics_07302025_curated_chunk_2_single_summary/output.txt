## 1. Executive Branch Actions And Domestic Policy

### 1.1 Economic Policy, Trade, and Growth

The administration is moving aggressively on its trade agenda, announcing a new 25% tariff on India. This measure reportedly includes an additional "penalty" tied to India's trade relationship with Russia [1]. President Trump has publicly confirmed that a related Friday tariff deadline "will not be extended," a firm stance that has effectively dashed any remaining hopes for a U.S.-India trade deal by the August 1 target date [2, 3].

These significant trade actions coincide with conflicting macroeconomic signals. Multiple reports highlight a strong economic rebound in the second quarter, with the U.S. economy growing at a 3.0% GDP rate [4]. However, a separate analysis from The New York Times covering the first half of 2025 indicates an economic slowdown, with the report concluding that the administration's tariff policies have had the effect of scrambling the underlying economic data, making clear assessment difficult [5].

### 1.2 Environmental And Labor Regulations

The administration is pursuing a foundational shift in federal environmental policy. The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) is reportedly planning to overturn the scientific "endangerment finding," the core legal predicate that enables the regulation of carbon and other greenhouse gas emissions [1]. According to one report, this move is an explicit attempt to halt the federal government's ability to regulate such emissions by choosing to "ignore science" [2]. Another headline from Rolling Stone characterizes the effort as "Gaslighting America in the Face of Climate Disasters" [3]. In a separate regulatory matter concerning labor, a proposed federal heat standard designed to protect workers has reportedly "languished" without action [4].
