## 2. Immigration Policy and Enforcement

### 2.1. Administration Launches Nationwide Vetting of 55 Million Visa Holders
The Trump administration has launched a vast immigration enforcement initiative, described as a "growing crackdown" [1]. The core of this operation is a systematic review of all 55 million active U.S. visa holders to identify individuals with deportable offenses. To facilitate this surge, Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) is reportedly investing millions in specially equipped vehicles for a significant operation in the Washington, D.C. area, signaling a logistical ramp-up to support the broad vetting policy [2].

A federal judge has ordered the complete closure of a controversial immigrant detention facility in Florida, colloquially known as "Alligator Alcatraz" [1]. The ruling, which cites environmental risks, requires the center to be fully shut down within 60 days and immediately prohibits any new detainees from being sent there [2]. This judicial action dismantles a significant piece of the administration's detention infrastructure in Florida.
