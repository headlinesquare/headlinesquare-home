The nation is in a state of high alert following Iranian retaliatory missile strikes on U.S. military bases in the Middle East, a direct response to recent U.S. military action. ## 1. Foreign Policy & National Security: The U.S.-Iran Crisis

### 1.1. Iranian Retaliation & Regional Escalation

Overnight, Iran launched a significant retaliatory missile attack targeting U.S. military interests in the Middle East. High-credibility reports confirm that missile strikes have hit U.S. air bases in Qatar and Iraq [1, 2]. Iranian state media quickly claimed responsibility for the assault [3]. The situation on the ground remains highly fluid and dangerous. Preceding the attack, explosions were witnessed in the skies over Qatar, which prompted the Qatari government to temporarily shut down its airspace and the U.S. to issue an urgent "shelter in place" warning to all American citizens in the country [4, 5]. While the full extent of the damage is still being assessed, the direct targeting of U.S. assets marks a severe and deliberate escalation of the conflict.

The Iranian retaliation follows U.S. military action that has pulled the nation back into active conflict in the Middle East [1]. President Trump has publicly claimed the initial American strikes caused "monumental damage" to Iran and has reportedly been agitating for "regime change" [2, 3]. However, there is some evidence suggesting a degree of strategic restraint, as satellite imagery indicates U.S. forces may have avoided striking Iran's nuclear reactors [4]. The crisis could expand rapidly; Israeli officials have stated that a U.S. decision on whether to join a broader war could be made "in days" [5]. The administration’s public framing of the conflict is also facing scrutiny, with one report accusing President Trump and media personality Sean Hannity of setting off a "wave of disinformation" after the bombing [6].

The diplomatic fallout is growing, with reports that multiple countries are withdrawing from an upcoming NATO summit, citing the "reckless" nature of the U.S. action [3]. In a notable reversal, a nation that previously nominated President Trump for a Nobel Peace Prize is reported to have "Dramatically Change[d] Its Tune" [4]. Further complicating the diplomatic landscape, President Trump has publicly clashed with Russia's Medvedev over claims that other nations might provide nuclear warheads to Iran [5].
