In a separate investigative thread, a CBS News report states it has uncovered "new discrepancies" in the jail video connected to Jeffrey Epstein's death, though the specifics of these findings are not detailed in the headlines [9]. These parallel tracks—Maxwell's conditional offer, her rejection by Congress, and the President's public statements and potential pardon considerations—are creating a highly volatile and unpredictable legal and political situation.

## 2. Executive Branch: Domestic Policy & Governance

### 2.1. Administration Moves to Repeal Foundational Climate Regulations

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) is spearheading a fundamental reversal of established U.S. climate policy. Multiple reports confirm the EPA is officially seeking to repeal the long-standing legal finding that greenhouse gas emissions, such as carbon dioxide, constitute a threat to public health and welfare [1]. This is being described as a "major deregulatory move" with far-reaching consequences [2]. The explicit goal is to dismantle the core legal underpinning established by the Clean Air Act, which for over a decade has served as the basis for the federal government's authority to regulate climate pollution [3, 4]. If successful, this repeal would effectively eliminate the existing federal framework for broad climate regulation.
