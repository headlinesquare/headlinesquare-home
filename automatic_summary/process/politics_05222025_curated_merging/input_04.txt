## 1. Legislative and Economic Policy

### 1.1 Republican Tax Bill ("Big, Beautiful Bill")

Republican leadership has secured passage of the "Big, Beautiful Bill" tax legislation. The White House has indicated President Trump wants to primary Republicans who voted against it [1]. Analysis from the Center on Budget and Policy Priorities indicates the bill disproportionately benefits wealthy taxpayers while costing more than extending the 2017 tax law [2]. Kentucky Governor Andy Beshear has called the legislation "cruel" and "wrong" [3].

Beyond tax provisions, the bill contains unrelated items including provisions on gun silencers and tanning beds [4]. The bill's passage was notably affected by the deaths of three Democratic lawmakers this year, whose votes would have been sufficient to block the legislation [5].

The Trump administration has barred Harvard University from enrolling international students [1]. This directive would force the transfer of foreign students currently at Harvard to other institutions [2]. In a separate but potentially related development, a federal judge has blocked the Trump administration from revoking international students' legal status more broadly [3].

## 3. Social Policy and Healthcare

### 3.1 Public Health and Healthcare Access

Health and Human Services Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr.'s Make America Healthy Again (MAHA) Commission has released a report on U.S. children's health [1]. The report specifically targets food, drug, and technology industries as contributing factors to health problems [2].

Federal support for scientific research has reportedly been reduced to its lowest level in decades according to The New York Times [3].

Republicans have proposed increasing administrative requirements for Medicaid recipients, characterized by critics as "burying users in paperwork" [4]. The House has passed a tax bill that prohibits Medicaid from covering transition-related care for transgender individuals and prevents Affordable Care Act marketplace plans from classifying transgender care as an essential health benefit [5].

## 4. Executive and Judicial Developments

### 4.1 Supreme Court Decisions

The Supreme Court has issued a ruling expanding presidential authority, allowing President Trump to fire leaders of independent regulatory agencies [1]. This decision specifically enables the termination of heads of independent labor agencies [2]. The Court created an exception for the Federal Reserve, which remains insulated from this expanded firing authority [3]. The ruling is characterized as temporary, with the phrase "for now" appearing in multiple news reports.

Interior Secretary Doug Burgum has directed the National Park Service to install signs flagging "negative" depictions of history at national parks [2]. The Federal Trade Commission has opened an investigation into Media Matters, a progressive media watchdog organization [3].

The White House has reduced transparency by replacing written transcripts with videos [4] and now withholds 99.5% of Trump's transcribed remarks, up from 80% previously [5]. Trump has promoted theories suggesting autism's rising diagnosis rates must be "artificially induced" by external factors [6].
