## 1. Executive Branch and Domestic Policy

### 1.1 Nationwide "Crime Crackdown" and Federal-Local Tensions

The administration has initiated a significant domestic law enforcement operation, described as a "crime crackdown," by mobilizing a large contingent of the National Guard [1]. As many as 1,700 troops are being deployed across 19 states. A central and highly visible component of this strategy is the directive to arm National Guard members stationed in Washington, D.C. [2]. However, the tactical implementation of the D.C. deployment is already drawing scrutiny, with reports indicating that troops are being positioned in some of the city's "safest sites," raising questions about the allocation of resources relative to the stated mission [3]. The initiative is also creating substantial friction with state leadership. After President Trump signaled that Chicago could be the next city targeted, Illinois Governor Pritzker issued a sharp public rebuke, stating, "We don’t play those games" [4]. The political tensions were further underscored when Vice President JD Vance, visiting troops in D.C., was heckled by protestors, whom he then publicly condemned as a "bunch of old primarily white people" [5].

### 1.2 Immigration Detention and Enforcement

Immigration enforcement is proceeding with a heavy reliance on military personnel and repurposed infrastructure. Reports indicate that 1,700 National Guard troops are being deployed across 19 states specifically for immigration purposes [1]. In a highly controversial move, the administration is now utilizing a U.S. Army base—once used for the internment of Japanese Americans during World War II—as what is being reported as the nation's largest ICE detention center [2, 3]. On an operational level, the Director of ICE has signaled a future enforcement presence at schools in Washington D.C., stating that while agents will not be present on the first day, they may be there "going forward" [4]. Operational logistics also appear to involve the regular use of Coast Guard aircraft to transport immigrants [5].

Beyond this specific deal, the administration's broader tariff strategy is causing significant international friction. In a direct response to new U.S. tariffs, multiple European postal services have reportedly halted all mail and package shipments to the United States, creating a tangible disruption in international commerce and logistics [3].
