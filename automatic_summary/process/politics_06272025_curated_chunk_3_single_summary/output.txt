## 1. Judiciary and Supreme Court Rulings

### 1.1. Supreme Court Limits Nationwide Injunctions, Bolstering Executive Power
The Supreme Court has delivered a landmark ruling that significantly curtails the ability of lower courts to issue nationwide injunctions [1]. This decision has immediate and direct consequences for executive policy, most notably by lifting the injunctions that had previously halted President Trump's plan to alter birthright citizenship [2]. Multiple reports characterize the ruling as a broad expansion of presidential power, as it limits a key judicial tool used to block administration policies on a national scale [3]. The administration has publicly lauded the decision, with President Trump and Attorney General Pam Bondi predicting it will "unblock [the] MAGA Agenda." While the ruling gives the administration a major procedural victory, it does not resolve the underlying legal questions surrounding the executive branch's authority to end birthright citizenship, leaving the core issue in legal limbo pending further litigation [4].
