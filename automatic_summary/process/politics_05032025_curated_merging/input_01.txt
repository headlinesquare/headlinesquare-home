## 3. Immigration & Border Security

### 3.1 Deportation Initiatives & Enforcement
The Trump administration has developed comprehensive deportation initiatives, including plans to track approximately 450,000 migrant children through door knocks and DNA tests [18]. International negotiations are underway, with reports indicating talks with Rwanda to accept deportees from the United States [37]. The deportation campaign has led to American citizens crossing state lines seeking safety, described by some as "refugees" [87], while raising questions about the status of U.S. citizen children whose parents face deportation [90].

The administration has implemented a strict "catch and revoke" policy for visa holders, establishing a zero-tolerance approach to visa compliance [53]. Meanwhile, the Department of Justice has filed a lawsuit against Colorado and Denver over their so-called "sanctuary laws" [42], escalating tensions between federal immigration enforcement priorities and state/local policies.
