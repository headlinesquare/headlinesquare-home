## 1. The "Megabill" - Legislative Process & Domestic Policy Overhaul

### 1.1 Legislative Strategy & Passage
The administration's sweeping policy package, termed the "megabill," faced significant jeopardy in the House. Resistance from a block of Republican holdouts stalled the bill late into the night, leaving its outcome uncertain [1]. House leadership, seeing the bill at risk, leaned heavily on President Trump, describing him as their "best closer" [2]. The President then engaged in a "charm offensive," personally lobbying dozens of dissenting members [3]. This direct intervention proved decisive and was credited with flipping key votes, such as that of Ohio Rep. Warren Davidson [4]. Ultimately, the bill overcame the internal party friction and cleared a critical procedural hurdle [5]. Democratic opposition has been fierce, with Michigan Rep. Tlaib condemning the measure as a "budget betrayal" that enacts the "largest cut to Medicaid and food assistance in history to give billionaires a tax break" [6].
