## 6. Economic and Trade Policies

### 6.1 Tariffs and Business Impacts

Trump's tariff policies are affecting businesses, consumers, and international relations. A used car business in Oklahoma is "already straining" from the tariffs [1], while wedding dress retailers face higher costs due to China tariffs, threatening small shops [2]. Financial analysts are questioning how much the tariffs will cost American families [3], as The Guardian characterizes warehouse and transport workers as "the first victims of a class war" from Trump's "shock therapy" [4].

The administration is expected to announce pharmaceutical tariffs "in next two weeks" [7] and has called for a 100% tariff on foreign films shortly after actor Jon Voight proposed "limited" tariffs [8]. This has prompted California Governor Gavin Newsom to ask Trump to work with him on a $7.5 billion tax credit for Hollywood [9], promoting the slogan "Make America Film Again" [10].
