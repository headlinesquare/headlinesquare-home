## 1. Executive Branch: Actions & Controversies

### 1.1 The Trump-Musk Feud Escalates, Threatening Economic and Political Stability

An intense public conflict has erupted between President Trump and his former tech czar, Elon Musk, originating from Musk's criticism of a Republican budget and tax plan [1, 2]. What began as a policy disagreement has rapidly devolved into direct threats and political maneuvering. The President has responded with escalating rhetoric, publicly suggesting he might deploy the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE)—a department he has termed a "monster"—to "go back and eat Elon" [3]. President Trump explicitly linked this threat to Musk's extensive portfolio of federal subsidies, stating, "Elon may get more subsidy than any human being in history," and suggesting DOGE could be unleashed to review them [4]. The feud has sharpened to the point where, when asked directly about deporting the South African-born Musk, the President stated he would "take a look" at the possibility [5].

In response to the President's actions, Musk has reportedly vowed to financially support a "nemesis" of President Trump in future political contests and has taken the further step of publicly proposing the formation of a new political party [6, 7]. The financial markets have reacted to this instability, with one report noting that Tesla's stock "tumbled" immediately following the President's threats [8]. Musk's proposal for a new "America Party" has been met with skepticism in media commentary, with one op-ed suggesting it is "doomed" [9].
