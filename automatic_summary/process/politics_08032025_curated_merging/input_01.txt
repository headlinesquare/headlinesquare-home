## 3. Economy, Technology, and Environment

### 3.1 Crisis Over Integrity of Federal Economic Data
A major political and institutional crisis is unfolding over the independence of federal economic data. President Trump fired the chief of the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the agency responsible for the nation’s core jobs and inflation data [1]. The move followed the President's accusations of manipulated jobs numbers, which former Treasury Secretary Larry Summers described as "preposterous" [2]. The firing provoked immediate and sharp condemnation from "Conservative Senators, Experts," who warned of the historical perils of politicizing official data collection [3, 4]. In the face of this bipartisan backlash, White House economic adviser Kevin Hassett publicly defended the President's decision. In the same period, Hassett has also acknowledged he could be a candidate to be the next Chair of the Federal Reserve, a development that raises further questions about the future independence of U.S. economic institutions [5].
