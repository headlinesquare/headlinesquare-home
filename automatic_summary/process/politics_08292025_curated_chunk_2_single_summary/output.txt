## 1. Executive Branch Power and Operations

### 1.1 Conflict with Congress over Spending and Budgetary Power

A significant conflict over constitutional spending authority is escalating between the White House and Congress. The Trump administration has moved to unilaterally block $4.9 billion in foreign aid previously approved by lawmakers, challenging the legislature's power of the purse [1]. To achieve this, the administration employed a "pocket rescission," a rare executive tool one report notes has not been used in nearly 50 years [2]. This action is being widely characterized by observers as an "end-run around Congress' funding power" [3]. The maneuver is interpreted as part of a broader strategy by the President to "wrest spending power from Congress" and has predictably generated sharp "pushback from Capitol Hill" [4, 5]. This power struggle is unfolding under the shadow of a potential government shutdown, with some analyses suggesting the President's gambit brings the nation "one step closer" to that outcome [6].

Two major economic developments have been reported with direct impacts on consumers and businesses. The core inflation rate rose to 2.9% in July, marking its highest point since February of this year and signaling persistent price pressures in the economy [1]. On the trade front, the administration has made a significant and abrupt policy change by eliminating the long-standing tariff exemption for imported parcels valued under $800. This ends the "duty-free no more" era for many small international shipments, a move with broad and immediate implications for consumers, small businesses, and the e-commerce sector [2].
