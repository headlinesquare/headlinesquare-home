### 1.2 Supreme Court and Legal Challenges

The Supreme Court has issued a temporary restraining order blocking the Trump administration from deporting Venezuelan migrants under the Alien Enemies Act [1], a rarely-used wartime law. The administration responded by characterizing the litigation as "meritless" [2]. The Court is also set to hear challenges to the Affordable Care Act's preventive care mandate [3] and arguments on Trump's attempt to ignore the 14th Amendment regarding birthright citizenship [4].

Justice Department policies under Attorney General Pam Bondi include severe penalties for Tesla vehicle vandalism, with promises of "decades behind bars" for perpetrators [5]. Simultaneously, the judiciary is pushing back on detention practices, with a federal judge ordering Rumeysa Ozturk's transfer from Louisiana to Vermont [6], explicitly stating that "the government cannot undermine the justice system and attempt to manipulate a case's jurisdiction."

A conservative judge in the Kilmar Abrego Garcia case has issued what is described as a "wake up call for America," warning of imminent threats to democracy [7].
