### 1.2 Major Shifts in Science, Health, and Environmental Policy

The administration is pursuing a suite of controversial policy changes across science, health, and the environment. President Trump has personally directed the termination of key NASA missions critical for monitoring climate change, including those that measure atmospheric carbon dioxide and global plant health [1]. This move is paralleled by a statement from Energy Secretary Chris Wright, who suggested that previously published government climate reports are being actively altered [2]. In health policy, plans are underway to curtail abortion access for veterans through VA hospitals [3]. Concurrently, an Associated Press fact-check has challenged the scientific basis of HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr.'s reasoning for cutting mRNA vaccines, stating the claims "are not supported by evidence" [4]. Climate science is further impacted by budget cuts at NOAA, which reports indicate are hampering the agency's ability to forecast rapidly intensifying hurricanes [5].
