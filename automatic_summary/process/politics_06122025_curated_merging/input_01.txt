### 1.1 Military and Law Enforcement Deployment

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has publicly defended the deployment of military personnel in Los Angeles, insisting the operation is lawful [1]. This lack of a clear legal foundation raises significant questions about the constitutional basis for using military forces in a domestic context [1] [2].

Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) is escalating its response to nationwide protests, planning to deploy tactical units in major U.S. cities [3]. This deployment represents a coordinated federal approach to civil unrest that extends beyond conventional immigration enforcement activities [3] [4]. The nationwide spread of protests indicates that the unrest is not confined to a single region [3] [5].

At the state level, Texas Governor Greg Abbott has mobilized the Texas National Guard, directing them specifically to locations where immigration protests are planned [6]. This deployment demonstrates the involvement of state-level resources in managing immigration-related civil unrest [6] [7]. The use of National Guard troops for responding to planned protests rather than for an ongoing disturbance suggests a preventative approach to potential civil disorder [6].
