## 1. Economic Policy and Trade
### 1.1 Tariff Implementation and Market Impact

The Trump administration has implemented reciprocal tariffs with significant exemptions for electronic goods including smartphones, computers, and microchips [1] [2]. Despite these carve-outs, the auto industry faces ongoing 25% tariffs expected to reduce sales by millions of vehicles and cost approximately $100 billion [3]. Companies are actively adjusting supply chains, with Apple airlifting 1.5 million iPhones from India to avoid tariffs [4].

Financial markets are experiencing significant volatility in reaction to the administration's economic policies. Currency markets appear particularly affected, with reports that while Trump may stabilize stock markets, "bond and currency markets are not so easily impressed as they rapidly de-dollarize" [5]. Manufacturing reshoring efforts would potentially increase consumer costs significantly, as exemplified by CNBC's examination of the potential price of a "Made in the USA" iPhone [6].

American farmers face economic pressure from both Chinese trade retaliation and domestic policy changes. China has imposed steep tariffs on U.S. exports, causing concern among farmers [7], while federal agricultural program cuts further threaten their income [8].
