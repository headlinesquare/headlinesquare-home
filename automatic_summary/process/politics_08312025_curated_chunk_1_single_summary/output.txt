## 1. Executive Governance and Administration

### 1.1. Federal-State Confrontation on Immigration Policy

A direct federal-state confrontation over immigration policy is escalating in Illinois, centered on the city of Chicago. The Mayor of Chicago has issued a formal executive order to resist a potential or reported federal immigration crackdown, which the mayor described as a response to "escalating threats from the federal government" [1]. The order explicitly directs city police to refuse assistance to federal troops or agents involved in such operations [2, 3]. This local-level defiance has elevated the conflict to the state and national stage. Illinois Governor JB Pritzker delivered a public address concerning the reported Trump administration plan to deploy military forces in Chicago [4], while other Democratic leaders have publicly questioned the federal government's legal authority for the proposed actions [5]. This series of events marks a significant challenge to federal authority and sets the stage for a potential legal and operational clash between city, state, and federal entities.

The Guardian reports that President Trump is actively intervening to protect his congressional allies, leading to accusations that these measures are an effort to "rig the midterms" [4]. A proposed ban on all mail-in voting is also part of this effort, though it may encounter resistance from within the Republican Party itself [5].
