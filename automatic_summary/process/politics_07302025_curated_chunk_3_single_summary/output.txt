## 2. Economic Policy & Trade

### 2.1 Aggressive New Tariffs and International Trade Disruption
The White House has unilaterally enacted significant and disruptive new trade policies with immediate, far-reaching market impacts. First, the long-standing "de minimis" tariff exemption for global low-cost goods and small packages has been officially terminated. This action is widely expected to increase the direct cost burden on American consumers by applying tariffs to a vast new category of imported goods [1]. Alongside this, the administration has imposed a universal 50% tariff on all copper imports, a move that caused the metal's global price to plunge immediately upon announcement [2].

These protectionist measures have drawn sharp warnings from the economic community. One Dartmouth College economics professor has labeled the administration's strategy a "tariff disaster," citing historical precedent [3]. While there is some evidence that the U.S. economy grew more than expected as the tariffs took effect, this data is clouded by a separate, alarming report from The Wall Street Journal. The investigation asserts that U.S. inflation data now relies "more on guesswork than previously known," raising fundamental questions about the integrity of the key federal metrics used to guide economic policy [4, 5].

### 2.2 Clashes Over Fiscal and Monetary Policy
Significant policy disagreements are erupting between the White House and other key economic bodies, as well as within the administration itself. In an extraordinary instance of internal dissent, a major administration proposal for newborn savings accounts has been met with extraordinary public criticism from Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent. The Secretary characterized the plan as a "'back door for privatizing Social Security,'" a politically explosive charge coming from a sitting cabinet member [3]. Separately, the state of Colorado is facing a major budget crisis, with officials reporting a $1 billion shortfall directly attributed to the fiscal consequences of a recent federal tax overhaul [4].
