## 3. Healthcare and Social Programs

### 3.1 Healthcare Policy Changes

Healthcare policy has emerged as a significant issue, with analyses suggesting it presents political challenges for the president [1]. Medicaid has become a central focus, with warnings that cuts would affect even non-enrollees due to the program's broader impact on healthcare infrastructure [2]. Divisions have appeared within the Republican Party, with some Republican senators publicly criticizing House-proposed changes to Medicaid as "cutting benefits" [3]. Veterans healthcare funding has become a focal point of efficiency debates, with advocates arguing that directly funding the VA would be more effective than expanding privatized care options [6].

Concerns persist about supply chain disruptions, with warnings of "empty ports, empty shelves" [3]. North Dakota enacted the nation's first law shielding Roundup's manufacturer from certain cancer lawsuits [4], while SpaceX lost a bid to control beach access near its launch facility in Texas [5]. Economic assessments include analyses of America's competitive position against China, with the Financial Times arguing that "Trump's unreliable America is throwing away the assets it needs" [6]. Tax policy trends show "new tax cuts mostly favor the rich across states this year" [7].
