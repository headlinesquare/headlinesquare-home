## 1. US-Iran Escalation and Ceasefire

### 1.1 Ceasefire Announcement and De-escalation

The immediate crisis with Iran appears to have culminated in a ceasefire. President Trump announced an agreement between Iran and Israel, a development confirmed by major news outlets [1]. This announcement capped a period of rapid de-escalation that began with the President's public framing of Iran's preceding military actions. Following Iran's attack on a U.S. base, President Trump characterized the retaliatory strike as "very weak," noting that Tehran had provided "'early notice'" [2]. In a highly publicized statement, he also explicitly thanked Iran for its “‘very weak’ response” to U.S. airstrikes, a move that appears to have significantly altered the trajectory of the conflict [3].

Prior to the de-escalation, Iran launched retaliatory missile attacks directly targeting a U.S. military base in Qatar [1, 2]. The direct military confrontation sharply increased regional volatility, prompting Gulf neighbors to close their airspace to civilian and commercial traffic [3]. The action was not entirely unexpected; the FBI had previously issued a public warning that retaliation from Iran was a distinct possibility following the initial U.S. strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities [4].

### 1.3 US Airstrikes: Justification, Legality, and Execution

The intelligence basis and legality of the U.S. airstrikes that precipitated the crisis remain subjects of intense debate. Citing U.S. intelligence, Senator Markwayne Mullin stated that Iran had not moved nuclear material from its Fordo facility before the attack [1]. However, Vice President JD Vance subsequently hinted that uranium might have been moved after President Trump’s public statements potentially tipped off Tehran [2]. This has fueled internal concerns, with one report alleging that some military officials viewed the President as the "'biggest threat' to operational security" and planned the operation around him [3]. Others question if the attack "completely missed its mark," contributing to the escalation [4]. The legality of the strikes is also facing challenges from legal analysts, who claim they violate both international law and the U.S. Constitution [5]. Concurrent with these events, the President has publicly floated the idea of pursuing regime change in Iran [6].

The administration has pushed back on Russian criticism, with President Trump publicly slamming Russia's Medvedev for suggesting other nations might provide Iran with nuclear warheads in response [3].
