## 1. Foreign Policy and National Security

### 1.1 U.S.-Russia Summit on Ukraine Faces Intense International Scrutiny

Intense and complex diplomatic maneuvers are underway ahead of a planned summit in Alaska between President Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin, focused on resolving the war in Ukraine. The geopolitical stakes are exceptionally high, with reports alleging that the U.S. and Russia are planning or suggesting a "West Bank-style occupation of Ukraine" [1, 2]. This potential framework has caused significant alarm among European allies, who have been conducting their own preparatory talks with both the Trump administration and Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy [3]. The core anxiety among EU leaders is that President Trump might offer "too much in exchange for very little" in his pursuit of a deal [4].

President Trump has projected a firm public negotiating posture, warning of "severe consequences" should President Putin not agree to halt the war [5]. In a conversation with French President Emmanuel Macron, President Trump reportedly affirmed his desire for Ukraine to have a direct say in any negotiations over its territory [6]. From Kyiv, President Zelenskyy has delivered a stark and direct warning to the American president ahead of the meeting, cautioning that on the key issues, "Putin is bluffing" [7]. The summit's outcome is highly uncertain, with allies watching for any signs of a major shift in U.S. policy that could reshape European security.

Mayor Muriel Bowser unequivocally condemned the action as an "authoritarian push," placing her in the difficult position of navigating the federal action while responding to congressional Democrats who have slammed the crackdown [1, 2]. In stark contrast, the city’s own police union is reportedly "cheerleading Trump's authoritarian takeover" [3].

The administration's allies have framed the move as a necessary response to public safety concerns, with Senator Mike Lee quoted as wanting the President "to go even further" in asserting federal control [4]. However, this narrative is being actively challenged, with at least one report describing the takeover as "hilariously ineffective" and disputing the premise for the action by asserting that D.C. is "not actually a crime-ridden hellhole" [5].
