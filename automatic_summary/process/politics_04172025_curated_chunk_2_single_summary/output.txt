One report detailed an effort to "Make Work Manly Again" by using tariffs as a tool to lure back manufacturing jobs traditionally seen as emblematic of national strength [1]. In a related vein, coverage indicates that while tariff measures have not yet resulted in higher prices on platforms like Amazon [3], there is an increasing chorus of concern over potential corruption linked to these policies [4]. Finally, international economic observers note that steps by the European Central Bank to reduce interest rates underscore the global ripples caused by America's tariff-driven strategy [6]. Collectively, these reports reflect an administration that is employing blunt economic tools amid a backdrop of considerable domestic and international uncertainty.

## 3. Federal Reserve and Monetary Policy Feud

A highly charged confrontation has erupted between the President and the Federal Reserve, marking an unprecedented chapter in U.S. monetary policy debates. The President has repeatedly called for drastic measures, exhorting the Fed to cut rates and even demanding that Fed Chair Jerome Powell be terminated—a level of executive intervention unseen in recent history [1]. Subsequent reports have detailed an escalating dispute in which the President's rhetoric has not only targeted Powell's policies but has also questioned the independence of the entire Federal Reserve system [2]. Several major news outlets have documented Powell's resistance to such measures, framing the situation as a major challenge to established monetary governance [3]. The President's recent calls, described in blistering terms by NPR and others, further add to the perception of a deep political rift over how best to manage the economy amid rising uncertainty [4]. Moreover, ongoing coverage indicates that despite the verbal onslaught, institutional confidence in Powell's leadership remains firm, as noted by Politico's analysis of the unfolding dispute [5]. This saga continues to expose the tension between politically driven economic directives and the safeguarded autonomy of financial policy experts.
