The ruling drew a forceful dissent from Justice Ketanji Brown Jackson, who wrote that the Court is actively "stymying" civil rights. She argued the decision strips recipients of "a deeply personal freedom: the ‘ability to decide who treats us at our most vulnerable’" [6].

## 3. Congress and Legislative Affairs

### 3.1. GOP Legislative Agenda Stalls in Senate

The Republican majority’s signature legislative package, the "big, beautiful bill," has suffered a critical setback. The Senate parliamentarian has ruled that a key provision involving significant cuts to Medicaid does not comply with the Byrd Rule for budget reconciliation, a procedural ruling that deals the bill a "major blow" [1, 2]. In a decisive announcement, Senate Majority Leader John Thune stated he will not use his authority to overrule the "Senate referee" on this matter, effectively blocking the Medicaid provision from the fast-track reconciliation process and jeopardizing the entire bill [3]. This comes as the Congressional Budget Office has already determined that the House-passed version of the bill would add trillions to the national debt, even when accounting for projected economic growth [4]. Further analysis from the Center for American Progress suggests older adults with ACA coverage would face "steep premium hikes" under the House bill [5].
