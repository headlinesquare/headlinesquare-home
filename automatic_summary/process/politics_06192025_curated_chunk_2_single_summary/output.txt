DAILY BRIEFING

The nation is navigating a period of acute and overlapping crises. The executive branch is publicly deliberating military action against Iran, with a two-week decision timeline and the contemplation of grave options creating significant global and domestic tension. This foreign policy emergency is compounded by escalating trade disputes, most notably a 30-day tariff ultimatum from Canada. Domestically, the administration’s aggressive immigration enforcement agenda is facing staunch resistance, marked by judicial injunctions, formal demands from state officials, direct confrontations between federal agents and members of Congress, and widespread public protest. Key social policies on LGBTQ rights and DEI initiatives are being contested in the courts and by community organizations. The administration itself shows evidence of internal friction, with high-level personnel reportedly at odds over foreign policy and a top intelligence official out of favor. Polling indicates majority public disapproval of U.S. involvement in foreign conflicts, presenting a potential obstacle to the administration's strategic objectives. This briefing synthesizes these fast-moving developments to provide actionable, non-partisan context for Congressional leadership.

## 1. Foreign Policy and National Security

### 1.1 U.S.-Iran Conflict Deliberations

The White House has put the nation on notice, publicly stating that a decision on U.S. military action related to the ongoing Israel-Iran conflict will be made within two weeks [1]. President Trump has personally signaled the potential gravity of such action, identifying the disabling of Iran's Fordo nuclear site as a move he sees as necessary [2]. While the President has issued a public denial of reports that he has already approved specific US attack plans [3], the deliberations are reportedly exploring the most extreme measures, with one report claiming the White House is contemplating the use of nuclear weapons against Iran [4]. The Pentagon is moving to a heightened state of readiness, preparing for a range of potential Iranian retaliatory actions, with a sharp focus on securing the critical Strait of Hormuz shipping lane [5]. The prospect of a strike is drawing significant opposition from Capitol Hill and the public. The top Democrat on the House Armed Services Committee has formally advised against military action [6], while veterans' groups are staging protests against what they term a "march to war" and are actively urging Congress to invoke the War Powers Act to halt any unauthorized attack [7].
