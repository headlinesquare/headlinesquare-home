## 1. Congressional Legislation & Domestic Policy

### 1.1 The congressional battle over the budget & tax bill

The Senate is locked in a high-stakes, marathon "vote-a-rama" session for the Trump administration's signature budget and tax proposal, referred to in headlines as the "'Big Beautiful Bill'" [1, 2]. The bill's passage is in serious jeopardy due to significant internal GOP opposition. At least five Republican senators are reportedly threatening a "McCain-like 'thumbs-down' moment," creating intense legislative drama [2]. Among the most vocal critics is Senator Thom Tillis (R-N.C.), who, described by one outlet as "unburdened by having to run for reelection," has publicly accused President Trump of "betraying" his own voters with the legislation [3].

Economic analyses of the bill from financial news sources indicate it heavily favors the wealthy. One report finds the richest 20% of Americans would receive an average income boost of $6,055 [4], while another outlet characterizes the bill as a "boon for the rich at the cost of the bottom 20%" [5]. The intensity of the prolonged legislative process is causing noticeable fatigue among senators. Senator John Fetterman was reported to have lamented missing a family beach trip and stated, “‘I just want to go home’” [6].

### 1.2 Bill’s social impacts & other legislative proposals

The Republican budget bill contains provisions for significant cuts to social programs. Reports indicate the GOP is set to cut billions from Medicaid [1]. These cuts could threaten the viability of approximately 300 rural hospitals nationwide and would be a significant burden on rural health clinics, according to one analysis [2, 3]. In a related development, Senator Thom Tillis is reportedly "defending Medicaid" even as he continues to "take shots at Obamacare" [4]. Beyond healthcare, the bill could also eliminate a key "financial lifeline" for some community college students [5].

Separately, a Senate bill, S.2205, has been introduced to "require a citizenship question on the decennial census" and modify the apportionment of Representatives to be based on the number of United States citizens instead of all individuals [6]. The status and support for this proposal are not detailed in available reports.

## 2. Immigration & Border Security

### 2.1 Federal-local conflicts & new enforcement directives

The administration has intensified its stance against local "sanctuary" city policies, filing a federal lawsuit against Los Angeles Mayor Karen Bass and the L.A. City Council over their sanctuary policies [1, 2]. One headline characterizes the move as President Trump declaring "War on Los Angeles" following recent protests targeting ICE [3]. This federal pressure contrasts with actions at the local level, where one California county is reportedly diverting funds from law enforcement to instead provide support for ICE detainees [4].

In a major policy shift, the Department of Justice issued a new directive to strip naturalized U.S. citizens of their citizenship if they have committed criminal offenses [5]. This emerges as a deal between President Trump and an individual named Bukele may reportedly threaten the administration's stated goal of dismantling the MS-13 gang [6].
