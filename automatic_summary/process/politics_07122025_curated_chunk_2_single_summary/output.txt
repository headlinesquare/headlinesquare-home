The federal response to the deadly floods in Texas is under severe scrutiny, focusing on both pre-disaster regulatory decisions by FEMA and critical operational failures during the crisis. Reports reveal that years before the flood, FEMA removed dozens of buildings at a Texas youth camp, "Camp Mystic," from the 100-year flood map. This de-regulation enabled the camp's expansion into what was a known flood hazard zone, where the disaster later struck [1]. During the emergency response, DHS Secretary Kristi Noem fired FEMA contractors, after which the agency reportedly missed thousands of calls from desperate flood victims, an action one report called "'unforgivable'" [2]. President Trump visited Texas to defend the response, which some local officials praised, but he also "lashed out" at critical questions from the media [3]. The disaster has revived public focus on the President's past statements about wanting to eliminate FEMA entirely [4].
