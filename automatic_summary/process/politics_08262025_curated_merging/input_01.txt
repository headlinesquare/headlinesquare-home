## 1. Economic, Trade, and Fiscal Policy

### 1.1 Conflict with Federal Reserve

The Trump administration has dramatically escalated its conflict with the Federal Reserve by announcing the immediate removal of Governor Lisa Cook [1, 2]. This move is being reported as a new front in the President’s battle for control over the U.S. central bank [3]. The administration's stated justification for the firing are allegations of mortgage fraud leveled against Governor Cook [4]. In a significant act of defiance that challenges the President's authority, Governor Cook has announced she will not recognize the dismissal and refuses to stand down from her post [5]. This confrontation creates profound uncertainty regarding the leadership and independence of the nation's monetary authority.

Significant policy and funding changes are being implemented across federal agencies, with direct impacts on social programs. The Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) has formally terminated a National Institutes of Health (NIH) program that was designed to diversify the biomedical workforce [1]. Following administration cuts targeting abortion providers, healthcare clinics in Maine are being denied Medicaid funds during an ongoing lawsuit [2]. Administration pressure is also being felt at the state level, where Michigan Medicine has reportedly halted all gender-affirming care for transgender youth, a decision news reports attribute directly to pressure from President Trump [3]. Concurrently, agency spending priorities are shifting, with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) set to purchase 1,000 new SUVs for $100 million [4]. These shifts occur as FEMA staff have issued internal warnings that severe budget cuts could compromise the nation's disaster response capabilities to a critical degree [5].

## 4. Legislative and Judicial Arenas

### 4.1 Judicial Rulings on Redistricting and Voting Laws

The battle over election laws is intensifying in the nation's courts. A judge in Utah has delivered a landmark ruling, striking down the state's current congressional map as an illegal partisan gerrymander and ordering it to be redrawn for the 2026 elections [1]. The ruling also reinstated voter-approved redistricting reforms that the state legislature had previously overturned [2]. In another critical case, the Louisiana Supreme Court is hearing a redistricting challenge that reports suggest could endanger a long-standing federal civil rights law [3]. Separately, the state of Mississippi has petitioned the U.S. Supreme Court, seeking to restore a grace period for mail-in ballots after a GOP-led effort sought to eliminate it [4].
