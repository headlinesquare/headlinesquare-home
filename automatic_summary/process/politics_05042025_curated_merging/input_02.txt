## 2. Government Restructuring and Efficiency

### 2.1 DOGE Performance and Federal Employment  
The Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE), led by Elon Musk, has fallen dramatically short of its ambitious savings targets. Initial projections of $2 trillion in government savings have been slashed to $160 billion [1], while DOGE's operating costs have reached $135 billion, resulting in net savings of just $25 billion—approximately 1.25% of the originally promised amount. Concurrently, a significant reduction in the federal workforce is underway with at least 15,000 Department of Agriculture (USDA) employees accepting voluntary resignation offers [2].
