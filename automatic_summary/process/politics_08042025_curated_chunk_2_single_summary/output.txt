### 5.1 Aggressive Tariff Policies Rattle International Trade

The administration's aggressive tariff actions are causing significant international and domestic friction. President Trump announced he will "substantially raise tariffs" on goods from India, explicitly linking the action to that country's continued purchases of Russian oil [1]. This assertive move comes as the European Union has opted to delay its own planned retaliatory tariffs on U.S. goods for six months, a decision intended to create a window for de-escalation and trade talks [2]. Domestically, the impact is raising alarms. U.S. small-business owners are warning that new tariffs on various goods will force them to raise prices, with one headline quoting the sentiment that "Everything is about to get really expensive," signaling potential inflationary effects [3]. In parallel, a former Canadian foreign minister has warned of rising U.S.-Canada trade tensions, describing the current situation as "bad news" for the bilateral relationship [4].
