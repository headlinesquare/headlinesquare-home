## 1. The Trump Administration: Policy And Personnel

The administration is driving a broad and rapid policy agenda through a series of executive actions and personnel changes across key federal departments.

### 1.1 Justice Department And Federal Law Enforcement In Turmoil

A sweeping and controversial reorganization of the Federal Bureau of Investigation is underway, sparking significant alarm. Multiple reports confirm the firing of senior officials, including a former acting director identified as Brian Driscoll, who was widely viewed within the agency as a "champion of rank-and-file staff" [1]. Among those pushed out is a senior agent who helped oversee the FBI's response to the January 6th Capitol attack. His removal is reportedly a direct result of his refusal to comply with an administration demand to provide the names of FBI agents who investigated the riot, an act described as resistance to political pressure [2].

This personnel shake-up is compounded by a contentious hiring decision at the Department of Justice. The DOJ has brought on a January 6th rioter who was previously pardoned by President Trump. This individual was reportedly recorded during the riot telling fellow rioters who were attacking police to "kill 'em" and was also heard berating officers as "Nazis" [3]. The move has drawn sharp criticism and raised questions about the politicization of federal law enforcement agencies.

### 1.2 Escalating Immigration Enforcement And Policy Shifts

The administration is aggressively reshaping immigration policy and enforcement. President Trump has signed an executive order mandating a new U.S. Census that explicitly excludes undocumented immigrants from the national enumeration, a move with significant implications for congressional apportionment and federal funding [1]. This action coincides with reports of new policies that are functionally equivalent to a return of "family separation in a new form," contributing to what analysts are calling a worsening nationwide "caregiving crisis" as a direct consequence of mass deportations [2, 3].

Operationally, Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) is taking notably aggressive measures to expand its reach. The agency is reportedly reopening old immigration cases, even those involving deceased individuals, in what lawyers describe as a poorly managed process [4]. To fuel its enforcement drive, ICE has also eliminated age restrictions for agent recruitment, signaling a desperate push to broaden its personnel pool [5]. The human impact of these policies is becoming starkly visible, with one report detailing the jailing and solitary confinement of a Children's Hospital chaplain by ICE [6], and another chronicling the struggle of a South Texas man and his family navigating the consequences of a wrongful deportation [7].

The administration is advancing a slate of policies targeting the armed forces, higher education, and social welfare programs. A new directive from the U.S. Air Force will deny retirement pay to transgender service members being separated from the service [1]. This policy is a direct reversal of a prior commitment made by the Air Force, which had assured that trans service members being purged under Trump's policies would be able to retain their benefits [2].

In the education sector, the President is set to sign an executive action that will require universities to hand over detailed admissions data categorized by race [3]. Some analysts view this as a significant escalation in what they describe as the administration's "war on higher education" and a prelude to further federal intervention in university autonomy [4]. Separately, concerns are growing that a forthcoming executive order on homelessness is being designed to criminalize it, a development described as "terrifying" by advocates [5].

As these policies are rolled out, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent has publicly acknowledged in a report that the administration's tariffs are ultimately paid by American consumers, a direct contradiction of frequent administration claims [3]. This admission came as President Trump held an Oval Office announcement to defend the U.S. economy with charts, an effort to counter recent job reports that showed "warning signs" [4]. Separately, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) under the Trump administration is reported to be considering new rules that would "supercharge corporate TV consolidation" [5].

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has publicly announced that Israel plans to "take over all of Gaza" to achieve its stated goal of completely destroying Hamas [1]. An opinion piece in the Boston Globe suggests these diverging paths are damaging the bilateral relationship, arguing that "Israel is losing America" [4].
