## 1. The Trump Administration: Policies & Actions

### 1.1 Directives Targeting Washington D.C.
The administration is taking direct action to alter the landscape of the nation’s capital. President Trump has issued a public demand for all homeless individuals to "immediately move out" of Washington D.C., stating the goal is to make the city "more beautiful" [1, 2]. One report directly links the directive to the President's observations while traveling to his golf course [3]. This policy has been framed by critics as a "war on unhoused D.C. residents," with the President quoted as saying, "'No Mr. Nice Guy'" in his approach [4]. These actions are occurring as legal experts discuss the complex, though potentially feasible, process for a federal takeover of the District of Columbia, a move that would fundamentally alter the city's governance [5].

This development coincides with the release of a new report from the office of Senator Jon Ossoff (D-GA), which has documented 510 "credible reports of human rights abuse" against individuals in immigrant detention facilities [4].

### 1.3 Health and Social Policy Directives
The administration is implementing major changes in federal health policy. HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy, Jr. has ordered a halt to certain federal research programs for mRNA vaccines [1]. This move prompted a stark warning from a former surgeon general who was appointed by President Trump; this former official stated that "people are going to die" as a consequence of the policy change [1]. In another significant policy shift, the administration is reportedly taking action to end abortion access for veterans in cases involving rape, incest, or the health of the mother [2]. Furthermore, a directive has been issued ordering all federal agencies to scrub their records of employee COVID-19 vaccination statuses [3]. Amid these changes, health advocates are reportedly in a race against time to secure federal maternal health funding before it expires [4].

## 3. Economic and Technology Policy

### 3.1 New Levy on Technology Sector and China Trade
The administration has implemented a direct and novel financial policy targeting the U.S. semiconductor industry's trade with China. A new directive requires American chip giants Nvidia and AMD to pay the U.S. government a significant portion of their earnings from the Chinese market. Reports from multiple financial news outlets indicate the figure is set at "15% of China chip sale income" or "revenues" [1, 2]. This move represents a direct federal intervention to capture a share of profits from high-tech exports to a key geopolitical and economic rival.
