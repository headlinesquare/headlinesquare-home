## 4. Elections & Voting Rights

### 4.1 The Texas Redistricting Standoff and Supreme Court Actions

A major constitutional conflict has erupted in Texas over redistricting. Democratic lawmakers have fled the state to block a GOP-led effort to pass a new congressional map, prompting Governor Greg Abbott to order their civil arrest [1, 2]. The Texas Republican-controlled legislature voted to authorize the arrests, escalating the standoff [3]. This state-level battle is mirrored by a national push and pull over election laws. In California, Democrats are moving to dismantle a GOP-friendly map, potentially cutting five Republican House seats in a direct response to the Texas situation [4]. At the federal level, the Supreme Court is setting the stage for what could be a significant weakening of voting rights protections in a case originating from Louisiana [5].
