## 1. Congressional Investigations and Actions

### 1.1. House Expands Epstein Probe, Subpoenas Clintons and Former Top Officials

The House of Representatives has expanded its investigation into the Jeffrey Epstein case, taking significant new steps to compel testimony and access government records. The House Oversight Committee has joined other congressional panels in issuing subpoenas for Justice Department files pertaining to the matter [1, 2]. ## 3. The Texas Redistricting Controversy

### 3.1. Crisis Escalates as Arrest Warrants Are Issued for State Democrats

The political standoff over redistricting in Texas has escalated into a direct legal confrontation involving the judiciary. A Texas judge has issued arrest warrants for the Democratic state lawmakers who departed the state to break quorum and prevent the passage of a Republican-led redistricting plan [1]. From their location outside Texas, the Democratic lawmakers held a press conference to defend their actions as a necessary last stand against partisan gerrymandering, warning that the Texas model could be replicated elsewhere at President Trump's encouragement [2]. President Trump has weighed in on the matter personally and repeatedly, publicly asserting that due to population growth, Texas Republicans are "'entitled' to five more seats" [3].
