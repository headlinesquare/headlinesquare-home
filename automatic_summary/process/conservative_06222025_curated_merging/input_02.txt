Their position, as articulated in a USA Today article referenced in a user post, is that the resolution grants the president the authority to commit U.S. forces for a 60-day period without prior and explicit approval from Congress, thus placing the current action within a legal framework [1]. This has broadened the discussion to include the actions of past presidents and whether they sought congressional approval for similar military actions [3].
