Concurrently, Iran’s President was seen leading public rallies in Tehran with chants of "Revenge, Revenge!" [5]. The conflict has also engaged regional proxies, with a reported threat against "American ships" emerging from Yemen [6].

[2, 3]. The administration directly linked the action to Israeli security, with President Trump asserting Israel is "much safer now" [8].

The legality of the President's action has become the central flashpoint of domestic conflict. The core of the Democratic opposition is the charge that the strikes were conducted "'Without Congressional Approval'" [1]. Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer broke his silence to formally oppose the attack [2], while Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez has explicitly "Calls for Trump to Be Impeached over U.S Strikes on Iran" [3]. This has elevated the discourse to a constitutional level, with headlines confirming that "Impeachable" is now a key part of the debate [4]. Counterarguments cite the War Powers Resolution of 1973, which a USA Today report notes "gives the president authority to act for 60 days without congressional approval" [5].
