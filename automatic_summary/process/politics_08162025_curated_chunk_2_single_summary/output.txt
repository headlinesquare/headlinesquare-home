## 1. Foreign Policy & National Security

### 1.1. The Trump-Putin Summit and Subsequent U.S. Policy Shift on Ukraine

A summit in Alaska between President Trump and President Putin has resulted in a significant shift in U.S. foreign policy toward the war in Ukraine. Following the meeting, the administration has reportedly aligned its stance with Russia's preferred path to a peace settlement, with President Trump now urging Ukraine to accept a Russia-backed plan [1, 2]. While administration officials emphasize the positive rapport, the summit concluded without a formal, announced deal, leading critics like Democratic Representative Swalwell to label the outcome the "Art of no deal" [3, 4].

Despite the lack of a formal pact, Moscow reportedly views the summit as a major strategic victory, as President Trump appears to have adopted President Putin's framework for ending the war [5]. The meeting has provoked a strong backlash, with Ukrainian sources expressing a profound sense of betrayal over the new U.S. posture [6]. The summit's transparency has also been questioned after government papers, allegedly containing details of private discussions, were discovered in an Alaskan hotel [7]. In a subsequent Fox News interview, President Trump reportedly focused not on policy specifics but on the personal flattery he received from President Putin [8].

## 2. Domestic Governance & Executive Power

### 2.1. Federalization of D.C. Policing and National Guard Deployment

The administration is significantly increasing the federal security footprint in Washington, D.C. by deploying more National Guard soldiers to the capital [1]. In a notable escalation, these troops, including units from West Virginia sent at President Trump’s direct request, have been authorized to carry weapons [2, 3]. This mobilization is occurring even as the administration has "partially" retreated from its plan for a complete federal takeover of the city's police force after striking an unspecified deal with local officials [4]. The heightened and armed federal presence has reportedly created an environment of fear, with local teenagers telling reporters that the federal police presence makes them feel unsafe [5]. The administration has not provided a clear public rationale for the troop deployment, prompting questions from media observers regarding the necessity and goals of the operation [6].

## 3. Immigration & Border Policy

The administration's accelerated deportation policy is forecast to have major economic consequences. According to one top economist, the large-scale removal of immigrant labor could cause domestic inflation to rise to 4% next year [1]. In a significant operational shift, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) has reportedly removed the Spanish-language training requirement for its new recruits [2]. The climate of intensified enforcement is having a direct impact on community life; organizers of a popular family festival in Charlotte, North Carolina, have canceled the event, explicitly citing widespread "ICE deportation fears" [3].
