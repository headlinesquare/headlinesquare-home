## 1. The Executive Branch

### 1.1 Federal Takeover of D.C. Policing Spurs Controversy
The administration has initiated what news sources describe as a "takeover" of policing in Washington D.C., marked by the arrival and operational deployment of National Guard troops [1]. This federal action has elicited sharp criticism, with opponents arguing the President's accompanying rhetoric "echoes a history of racist narratives about urban crime" [2]. The Mayor of Baltimore publicly slammed what he called the President’s “racist’ views of Black-led cities” in response [3]. This move is being watched with significant alarm by Congressional Democrats, who have stated concerns that the administration will use D.C. as a template to target other major U.S. cities [4].

The legal framework for such military deployments on U.S. soil remains a central point of contention. A federal court is currently hearing arguments focused on whether President Trump previously violated the Posse Comitatus Act by deploying troops to Los Angeles during protests. Underscoring the debate, sworn testimony from a U.S. general has revealed the L.A. deployment proceeded despite internal military assessments indicating a "low risk," raising new questions about the administration's criteria for domestic military intervention [5].

## 2. The Economy

### 2.1 Inflation and Tariffs Pressure Consumers and Businesses
Multiple reports indicate that U.S. inflation remains elevated as the administration's recently implemented tariffs "take hold" across the economy [1]. The direct impact of these tariffs on consumer costs was reflected in the consumer price index, which continued its rise in July [2]. Parallel to this, a new analysis of recently enacted GOP economic legislation concludes that the laws are structured to provide the largest benefits to the "Richest" Americans while resulting in the "Steepest Cuts" for the poorest [3].

The combination of rising food prices and potential cuts to the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) is reportedly creating severe economic pressure, with low-wage workers "reeling" from the dual impact [4]. This strain is not limited to households; a Michigan-based manufacturer has issued a public warning that the new tariff regime threatens the very survival of small businesses across the country, publicly contradicting administration messaging on economic prosperity [5].
